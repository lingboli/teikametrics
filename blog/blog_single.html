Building a more accurate Amazon Repricing engine with data imputation

Earlier in September, we announced the launch of our next generation Amazon Repricing solution code-named 'Bonchon'. A large part of our goal with Bonchon was to leverage a new method of sampling Amazon pricing data we beta tested with Amazon's MWS team. At the core of this new technology is the Amazon MWS Subscriptions API - a new and improved interface to Amazon pricing data.

As Teikametrics continues to focus on the development of advanced data-driven technology to give its clients a trading edge, a key asset continues to be our unique data warehousing and proprietary analytics platform. Whilst using complex database processes that are unique to Teikametrics to capture billion's of pricing data points, we've developed strategies that have dramatically improved the profitability for our clients and simultaneously learned a great deal about the quality of Amazon's data.

The Scale of Amazon.com

The Amazon River discharges more water than world's next seven largest rivers combined. Its scale is truly astounding. Amazon Inc.'s 'cloud', in the virtual world, is almost comparable to its namesake and natural wonder. Consider that Amazon's Web Service (AWS) cloud infrastructure is currently estimated to be five times larger than all of its major cloud infrastructure competitors combined. Amazon AWS is the heart of Amazon's broad array of products including EC2, a cloud storage solution that powers many of the largest consumer websites in the world including Foursquare, Zynga, and Amazon.com itself.

Amazon.com's data is flowing at an astounding rate. In 2012, it was estimated that:

  • Amazon.com sold over 300 items per second
  • across its catalog of over 200 million items.
  • The resulting torrent of data from Amazon.com's retail activity is managed globally

Data Quality issues using Amazon's API

The number of consumer internet sites that have activity comparable to Amazon.com are few and far between. Facebook.com is one of a very few sites that is larger and understanding its data synchronization challenges is helpful with respect to data quality across Amazon's network. Even with arguably the world's most advanced database warehousing and distributed infrastructure, a Facebook user often experiences slight glitches in data. For example, posting a picture or commenting on another user's wall is not registered in real-time by other users in your network. Sometimes this can take several minutes or perhaps only a few seconds in a different scenario. This inconsistency is a result of the technical challenge to handle the scale of millions of database calls initiating simultaneously across the Facebook application. Likewise, the application running Amazon.com's consumer experience frequently experiences delays, outages, and apparent data glitches for a small subset of the database operations that drive transactions, product updates, reviews, price adjustments, and thousands of other application actions that consumers and third-party retailers can execute.

For Teikametrics' clients, these 'glitches' can have a dramatic affect on performance if incorrectly handled by an automated repricing solution. During the new client consultation process, we often hear of short-falls in other repricing systems in the area of error handling. These short-falls can be as basic as a potentially fatal assumption that Amazon's API will always report accurate pricing data on-time, every-time. At Teikametrics, years of experience with the Amazon APIs and retail platform have taught us that it is not good enough to take anything for granted especially when valuable inventory is at stake. Any mission critical true enterprise scale software solution must account for potential issues an errors with a robust fail-safe process - an Amazon Repricing Platform is no different.

Solutions for Complex Data Problems

We're proud to have developed several layers of database technology to simultaneously identify potential data errors with the Amazon API and address them to ensure our clients position their products in the most competitive scenario possible. Data science techniques such as error correction and data imputation enable us to have confidence that Teikametrics can offer the most accurate and robust Amazon Repricing Platform to its clients.

What is Data Imputation?

Data Imputation is the process of 'filling in' missing data in a sample dataset. Teikametrics has developed proprietary technology to enable its clients to use Data Imputation to address any mismatches in Amazon API data. By closely monitoring our clients Amazon API responses, we're able to understand precisely how many API calls are complete, delayed, or mismatched and crucially execute adjustments to pricing logic to respond in the best possible way to each scenario. The result is an Amazon Repricing solution that is robust and scalable for high volume Amazon 3P retailers who require the highest level of performance.
